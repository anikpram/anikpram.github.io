Introduction
Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) provides an excellent soft tissue contrast at the expense of long acquisition times. MRI scan times are directly proportional to image resolu- tion, thus, warranting accelerated acquisition schemes. Paral- lel MRI (PMRI) was introduced for faster acquisition. It uses an array of coils, spatially sensitive to different regions of the organ of interest1, enabling undersampled acquisitions. PMRI reconstruction approaches can be classified into pre- calibrated, auto-calibrated and calibration-less, depending on the estimation of coil sensitivities. While, pre-calibrated SENSE2 obtains sensitivity information from additional cal- ibration scans, auto-calibrated GRAPPA3 estimates it from a fully sampled region in center of k-space.
